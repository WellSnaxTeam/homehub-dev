System Overview

BookGuard is a referring agent program operated by Insurance Specialties / WellSnax. Outside insurance agents refer clients to Insurance Specialties for products those agents are not licensed to sell — health, Medicare, life, and similar. Insurance Specialties handles the sale, and the referring agent earns a commission. The program gives outside agents a meaningful additional revenue stream without requiring them to expand their own licensing.

The system currently runs across two separate platforms that are not integrated with each other. GoHighLevel (GHL) handles CRM, automation, referral intake, and pipeline management. Business HQ (app.businesshq.pro) provides an agent-facing dashboard for referral tracking and commission visibility. There is no custom software. Everything is platform configuration layered over a largely manual process, and the two platforms do not share data.

Context for developersThe current system breaks frequently. The goal of the HomeHub integration is to replace this GHL setup with a proper custom-built solution that eliminates manual overhead, fixes automation gaps, and gives referring agents real visibility into their referrals.

End-to-End Workflow

The current process has 8 phases. Each phase involves some degree of manual action. There is no step that is fully automated without a human trigger somewhere in the chain.

  • 1
    Agent Relationship and Agreement
    Referring agent relationships are initiated through a variety of channels. Team members may connect with agents at conferences, through existing networks, or via direct outreach, and walk them through the benefits of joining BookGuard. The core value proposition for the referring agent is a trusted partner who can handle insurance products they are not licensed to sell, with a commission paid for each successfully placed referral.

    Once an agent decides to move forward, a BookGuard-specific referral agreement is sent for signature. This agreement exists alongside any prior commission arrangement and addresses the specifics of how BookGuard referrals are executed and compensated. The agreement may be sent by any team member coordinating the relationship. The signed document is stored and the agent is handed off for technical setup.
  • 2
    Agent Onboarding
    The agent is sent to bookguard.com/referring-agent to complete the onboarding form. Submission auto-adds them to GHL Contacts. The entry is manually verified and the agent is assigned to a Campaign.
  • 3
    Custom Landing Page Setup
    A custom funnel step is created in GHL, cloned from the master template, and customized with the agent's photo, name, phone, email, and website. Each agent gets their own dedicated page, which is then published.
  • 4
    Campaign Setup
    A new Campaign is created in GHL Marketing, named after the agent, linked to their custom funnel step, and published. Agent commissions are set to $0 in GHL since payouts are handled externally. If the agent is not yet in the Referring Agents list, they must be added manually before the campaign can go live.
  • 5
    Unique URL Generation
    The agent's unique referral URL is copied from the campaign. Format: https://lead.bookguard.com/[campaign]?am_id=[agent ID]. The Referring Agent Review Link on the agent's GHL contact card must be manually verified to have populated correctly before anything else can proceed.
  • 6
    Send Agent Their Materials
    The agent receives their unique landing page URL and a printed QR code for display in their office. The instruction to the agent is simple: when a client needs products you do not write, send them here.
  • 7
    Referral Intake
    The client visits the agent's landing page and submits the intake form. GHL creates a new Contact record. After approximately 5 minutes, an email notification fires to the ops team. An Opportunity record is created and enters the active pipeline for Insurance Specialties to follow up.
  • 8
    Manual Cleanup (When Automations Fail)
    When a contact lands in GHL without triggering the automation, the referring agent fields must be filled in manually, an Opportunity record created by hand, and the workflow triggered manually. This is not a rare edge case. It is a recurring part of operating the current system.

Automations

Critical limitationEach referring agent campaign requires its own individually configured automation trigger. Only one campaign trigger can be set up at a time. Every new agent added to the program requires returning to the Automations screen and manually adding a new trigger. If this step is missed or the UI changes break the process, leads arrive silently with no notification and no pipeline entry.

How triggers work

Each campaign uses a Lead Created trigger filtered by campaign name. Once the trigger fires, the notification email goes to the ops team and the Opportunity record is created. If the trigger was not configured before the lead came in, the lead goes to Contacts with no pipeline association and no notification. Nothing in the system flags that this happened.

Pain Points

These are documented, recurring failures in the current system. A critical characteristic of most of these issues is that they fail silently. No error is surfaced, no alert fires. Something simply does not happen, and no one knows until they go looking manually.

Reliability

Referrals fall into Contacts instead of triggering automations. This has happened with multiple confirmed submissions. The lead exists in the system but never enters the pipeline and no one is notified.

Reliability

Notification emails go to spam. Took multiple days to detect and resolve. The issue can recur with any new recipient added to the notification list.

Attribution

There is no reliable way to identify which agent sent a referral without manually checking. Ops staff confirmed during internal testing they could not determine the source from the notification alone.

Attribution

If an agent shares the wrong link or a client navigates directly to the page, the referral has no campaign association and cannot be traced back to the agent automatically.

Process

Every new agent requires 5 or more manual steps across GHL: funnel step, campaign, automation trigger, URL verification, and materials distribution. There is no shortcut.

Process

When a lead misses the pipeline, manual cleanup is required for every field. No automatic recovery exists. An operator must locate the contact, fill in agent fields, create an Opportunity, and manually trigger the workflow.

Agent Experience

An agent-facing dashboard exists in Business HQ, but no one documented how agents are set up on it or how to access it. As a result it goes largely unused. Agents still rely on manual updates for any information about their referrals.

Infrastructure

GHL UI changes have already broken the documented setup process. The step-by-step instructions no longer match the current interface, which increases setup errors and onboarding time.

What's Not in Place

These capabilities do not exist in the current GHL system and represent the target build scope for the HomeHub integration.

  • Automated agent notifications when a referral submits, is contacted, or converts
  • Agent-facing portal with real-time referral status tracking — a dashboard exists in Business HQ but has no documented onboarding process and agents are not routinely told how to access it; proper implementation is missing
  • Reporting on referral volume, conversion rate, and source attribution
  • Referral source attribution visible to non-admin users
  • Automatic submission confirmation sent to the referring agent
  • Automated onboarding flow for new agents replacing the current multi-step manual GHL process
  • Duplicate lead detection and prevention
  • Self-configuring automation triggers that do not require per-agent manual setup
Build directiveA dashboard exists in Business HQ but is disconnected from GHL and has never been properly rolled out to agents. The HomeHub replacement should handle all agent onboarding, referral intake, attribution, notification, and status tracking without requiring manual intervention at any step — and should provide agents with a clear, documented path to accessing their dashboard from day one. Consolidate what currently lives across two unconnected platforms into one coherent system.

Known System Issues

The following are documented failure patterns observed during operation of the current system. They are listed generically to illustrate the types of problems the HomeHub build should resolve.

  • Open
    Referral submitted but never entered the pipelineA client completed and submitted the intake form. The contact was created in GHL but the automation trigger did not fire. No notification was sent and no Opportunity was created. The referral was only discovered through manual follow-up. This pattern has occurred more than once.
  • Open
    Referral source could not be identified after submissionA referral entered the system but attribution to the originating agent was unclear. Ops staff confirmed they could not determine the source from the notification they received. The attribution was only resolvable by an admin examining the contact record directly.
  • Resolved
    Ops notification emails routed to spamNotification emails generated by GHL were delivered to spam for multiple days before the issue was identified. Resolution required manually whitelisting the sending domain. This could recur if the sending configuration changes or new recipients are added.
  • Open
    Duplicate contact record with no clear sourceA contact appeared in the system as a duplicate entry with no identifiable campaign association or referral source. No automation had fired and the origin of the record could not be determined.
  • Open
    Agent dashboard exists but has never been properly implementedBusiness HQ (app.businesshq.pro) provides an agent-facing dashboard that shows referral counts and is designed to support commission tracking. However, no onboarding process was ever documented or communicated to referring agents. Agents are not told how to access it, and the dashboard has not been incorporated into any setup or handoff workflow. The platform exists in isolation from GHL and is effectively unused.
  • Open
    Documented setup process no longer matches GHL interfaceThe step-by-step setup documentation no longer aligns with the current GHL interface. Menu locations and workflow options have changed, making the documented process unreliable for onboarding new agents without additional troubleshooting.
